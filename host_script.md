# THE TRAITORS: COMPLETE HOST SCRIPT

## 34 Players | 6 Traitors | 28 Faithful | 2 Houses Merging to 1

---

> **A NOTE TO THE HOST**
>
> This is your bible for the evening. Read the bold lines aloud. The regular text is stage direction and notes for your eyes only. Italicized lines in brackets are optional flavor you can add or skip based on pacing.
>
> You are not just a referee -- you are the narrator of this story. You are the castle itself, ancient and knowing, watching these mortals scheme and stumble. Channel Alan Cumming's silky menace, a Shakespearean narrator's gravity, and a game show host's showmanship. Pause for effect. Lower your voice. Let silence do the work.
>
> Keep a printed copy of this script, a pen for tracking eliminations, and a watch or phone timer nearby at all times.

---

# ============================================================
# ACT I: THE OPENING CEREMONY
# ============================================================

## SETTING THE STAGE

*Before players arrive: Dim lights. Queue atmospheric music (castle ambience, strings, something foreboding). Have role envelopes pre-sorted. Two areas designated for House A and House B. A central gathering area for the opening.*

*Players gather. Let them mingle for 5-10 minutes. Then kill the music. Step to the center. Wait for silence. If they don't quiet down, simply stand still and stare. They will.*

---

## THE WELCOME ADDRESS

**Good evening.**

*Long pause. Scan the room.*

**Good evening, and welcome... to this castle.**

**Thirty-four souls have gathered here tonight. You came as friends. You will leave... well. We shall see what you leave as.**

**Alexandre Dumas once wrote: "All human wisdom is contained in these two words -- Wait... and Hope." Tonight, you will do both. You will wait for the truth. You will hope it does not find you too late.**

**Because among you -- standing beside you, perhaps even smiling at you right now -- are six traitors.**

*Pause. Let that land. Watch faces.*

**Six people in this room have already decided to betray you. They know who they are. You do not. That is the game. That is the only game that matters.**

**Sun Tzu wrote in The Art of War: "All warfare is based on deception." And my friends, you have just walked onto the battlefield.**

*[Optional: gesture grandly at the room]*

**Tonight, we play The Traitors.**

---

## THE RULES OF THE CASTLE

**Before we begin, every castle has its laws. These are ancient. They are absolute. They are not negotiable.**

*Hold up fingers as you count:*

**One. When you speak during debate, you have thirty seconds. No more. When I signal, you stop. If you do not stop, I will stop you. This is not a suggestion.**

**Two. There is no whispering in this castle. Every word spoken is spoken to the room. If I catch whispers, I will call them out. The walls have ears -- and tonight, I am the walls.**

**Three. No phones. Your devices are dead to you until the night phase. If I see a screen, I will assume you are conspiring, and I will make your life... uncomfortable.**

**Four. When you are eliminated -- banished or murdered -- you are a ghost. Ghosts do not speak. Ghosts do not gesture. Ghosts do not mouth words or roll their eyes in a way that communicates information. You are dead. Act like it.**

**Five. My decisions are final. I am not a democracy. I am the host. As they say in The Godfather: "I'm going to make you an offer you can't refuse." And that offer is: play by my rules, or do not play at all.**

*Smile. Break the tension slightly.*

**Now. Shall we begin?**

---

# ============================================================
# ACT II: ROLE ASSIGNMENT
# ============================================================

## THE ENVELOPE CEREMONY

*Have 34 envelopes ready. Inside each: a card reading either "FAITHFUL" or "TRAITOR." Pre-sort so that each house gets exactly 3 traitors and 14 faithful. Mark the envelopes with House A or House B on the outside.*

**In my hands, I hold thirty-four envelopes. Inside each is a single word. One word that will define your entire evening.**

**As Shakespeare wrote: "There is no art to find the mind's construction in the face." You are about to discover whether that is true.**

**When I hand you your envelope, you will open it silently. You will look at the word inside. You will reveal nothing. Not a gasp. Not a smile. Not a flicker of anything. You will fold it, place it in your pocket, and you will wear the same face you are wearing right now.**

**If you are Faithful -- you are exactly what you seem. Your mission is to identify the Traitors among you and banish them before they destroy you all.**

**If you are a Traitor...**

*Pause.*

**...well. You already know what you are. Your mission is to survive. To deceive. To eliminate the Faithful one by one until you stand triumphant over their ruins.**

**As Iago whispers in Othello: "I am not what I am."**

**Let us find out who is what.**

---

*Walk through the room distributing envelopes. Do this slowly, deliberately. Make eye contact with each person. Say nothing. Let the silence build. After all envelopes are distributed:*

**Open your envelopes. Now. Silently.**

*Wait 10-15 seconds.*

**You have seen your fate. Destroy the evidence -- pocket it, tear it, eat it if you must. But from this moment forward, your face is your only shield.**

---

## THE HOUSE DIVISION

**Now -- this castle is ancient, and it has two wings. Tonight, you will be divided into two houses.**

*Gesture to the two designated areas.*

**When I call your name, move to your house. House A, to my left. House B, to my right.**

*Read names from your pre-sorted list. Alternate to keep it feeling random.*

**House A -- your seventeen souls will play together. House B -- your seventeen will do the same. You will not interact with the other house. Not yet. For now, your world is your house, and your house alone.**

**Within each house, three Traitors walk among you. Three out of seventeen. They know each other. You do not know them.**

*[Optional: "As Cersei Lannister once said: 'Everyone who isn't us is an enemy.' The question is... who exactly is 'us'?"]*

---

## SECRET TRAITOR BRIEFING

*This happens privately. Find a reason to pull traitors aside -- "I need to speak to a few of you about a logistical matter" or call them by name under the pretense of something mundane. Brief each house's traitors separately.*

### TO HOUSE A TRAITORS (Private)

**You three are the Traitors of House A. Look at each other. Remember these faces. These are the only people in this game you can trust -- and even that is temporary.**

**Your objectives:**
- **Survive banishment by deflecting suspicion.**
- **Each night, you will choose one player to murder. I will come to you during the night phase. You must agree on a target. If you cannot agree, I choose.**
- **During challenges, you may subtly sabotage -- but subtlety is everything. If you are obvious, you are dead.**
- **During the Chalice Ritual after the merge, ONE of you -- and only one -- may mark a player for death. I will explain this mechanic when the time comes.**

**As Michael Corleone said: "Keep your friends close, but your enemies closer." Your enemies are everyone else in that room. Smile at them.**

**Return to your house. Do not walk back together.**

### TO HOUSE B TRAITORS (Private)

*Deliver the same briefing.*

---

# ============================================================
# ACT III: PHASE 1 -- THE HOUSE ROUNDS
# ============================================================

## PHASE 1 OVERVIEW (HOST NOTES)

> Each house plays 3 rotations. Each rotation:
> - 5 min challenge
> - 10 min debate
> - 5 min vote (banishment)
> - 2 min night kill (murder)
>
> Run House A first, then House B, for each rotation. Or run them simultaneously if you have a co-host.
>
> After 3 rotations: 6 banishments + 6 murders = 12 eliminated. 34 - 12 = 22 players remain.

---

## TRANSITION TO HOUSE ROUNDS

**The game begins now. House A -- step forward. House B -- you will wait. Watch. Listen. Learn nothing useful.**

*[If running simultaneously: "Both houses -- to your wings. Your first challenge awaits."]*

**In each round, you will face a challenge. Then you will debate. Then you will vote to banish one among you. And then... night will fall. And the Traitors will feed.**

**Agatha Christie wrote: "Very few of us are what we seem." Let us test that theory.**

---

# PHASE 1: CHALLENGE INTRODUCTIONS
# --------------------------------------------------

## CHALLENGE INTRO: ROTATION 1 -- "THE CARTOGRAPHER'S TRIAL"

*This is the Central America countries challenge.*

**Your first trial. The Cartographer's Trial.**

**The old maps in this castle's library are crumbling, and the knowledge fades with them. Let us see how much geography survives among you.**

**The challenge is simple: Name the countries of Central America. You will go around the circle. Each person names one country. If you name a country that has already been said, or you name something that is not a country in Central America, or you hesitate for more than five seconds -- you are out of the challenge.**

**The last person standing wins safety. You cannot be murdered tonight.**

*[Optional: "As they say: 'Not all those who wander are lost.' But some of you are about to prove that very, very wrong."]*

**We begin... now.**

*Run the challenge. Keep it moving. Be theatrical about eliminations -- "Wrong! You have fallen!" or simply a slow head shake.*

---

## CHALLENGE INTRO: ROTATION 2 -- "THE HOUND'S KNOWLEDGE"

*This is the Dog Facts challenge. The birthday girl's dog is turning 5.*

**Your second trial. We call this one... The Hound's Knowledge.**

**Now, I understand we have a birthday to celebrate tonight, and a very important dog who is turning five years old. This challenge is dedicated to that noble beast.**

**I will read a series of questions about dogs -- some factual, some... creative. You will answer on paper. The player with the most correct answers wins safety from tonight's murder.**

*[Optional: "As the great philosopher Snoop Dogg once implied... actually, let us not go there. Pens ready."]*

**Question one...**

*Read your prepared dog fact questions. Mix legitimate facts with pop culture references. Award safety to the winner.*

---

## CHALLENGE INTRO: ROTATION 3 -- "THE SILENT LINE" (DIRECT DEATH CHALLENGE)

*This is the Direct Death Challenge. Players line up by age, oldest to youngest, WITHOUT SPEAKING.*

**Your third trial. And I must warn you -- this one has teeth.**

**This is The Silent Line. There is no safety to be won here. There is only survival... or instant death.**

**You will line up in this room in a single line. The order? Oldest to youngest. Date of birth. The catch?**

*Pause.*

**You may not speak. Not a single word. Not a whisper, not a murmur, not a syllable. You may gesture, you may point, you may hold up fingers, but your voice is forbidden.**

**You have three minutes to arrange yourselves.**

**When the time expires, I will check the line. The two players in the worst positions -- the most egregiously misplaced -- will be instantly banished. No debate. No vote. No mercy.**

**As they say in Game of Thrones: "When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die." There is no middle ground here.**

**Your three minutes begin... now.**

*Start timer. Watch carefully. When time expires:*

**STOP. Hands at your sides. The line is set.**

*Walk the line. Check ages. Find the two worst placements. Be dramatic about it -- inspect, squint, shake your head slowly.*

**You... and you. Step forward.**

*Address the two misplaced players:*

**The line has spoken. You were found wanting. You are banished. Immediately. There is no appeal.**

*Reveal their roles:*

**Turn over your cards. Show us what you were.**

*If Faithful:* **A Faithful soul, cut down by geography and silence. The castle mourns.**

*If Traitor:* **A Traitor! Exposed not by wit or accusation, but by the simple inability to stand in the right place. How fitting.**

---

## OPENING A DEBATE (Use one per round -- rotate through these)

### Debate Opener A

**The challenge is done. Now comes the harder game.**

**You have ten minutes to discuss. To accuse. To defend. To lie, if that is your nature. Remember: thirty seconds per person when you speak. I am watching the clock, and I am merciless.**

**Who was suspicious during the challenge? Who was too quiet? Who was too loud? As Hercule Poirot would say: "It is the brain, the little grey cells, on which one must rely."**

**The floor is open.**

### Debate Opener B

**And now we arrive at the part of the evening where friendships are tested and alliances crumble.**

**Ten minutes. Thirty seconds each. You are looking for Traitors. They are looking at you and smiling. Shakespeare wrote in Julius Caesar: "The evil that men do lives after them; the good is oft interred with their bones." What evil has been done here today? Discuss.**

**Begin.**

### Debate Opener C

**Challenge over. Masks on -- or off, depending on your persuasion.**

**Sun Tzu tells us: "If you know the enemy and know yourself, you need not fear the result of a hundred battles." But do you know the enemy? Do you even know yourself?**

**You have ten minutes. Use them wisely. Or use them to dig your own grave. The choice, as always, is yours.**

**Speak.**

### Debate Opener D

**The games within the game begin now.**

**Edmond Dantes spent fourteen years in prison before he understood who betrayed him. You have ten minutes. I suggest you work faster than he did.**

**The Count of Monte Cristo reminds us: "I am not proud, I am happy, and happiness blinds, I think, more than pride." Are any of you too happy tonight? Too comfortable? Too relaxed?**

**Discuss.**

### Debate Opener E

**Ten minutes on the clock. One banishment at the end.**

**Let me leave you with this thought from Agatha Christie: "The impossible could not have happened, therefore the impossible must be possible in spite of appearances." Someone in this room is not what they appear. Find them.**

**Go.**

### Debate Opener F

**We move now from the challenge to the conversation -- which is, I assure you, the far more dangerous arena.**

**As Don Corleone once said: "Friendship is everything. Friendship is more than talent. It is more than the government. It is almost the equal of family." But tonight, friendship is a weapon, and everyone in this room is armed.**

**Ten minutes. Thirty seconds each. Begin.**

---

## DEBATE FACILITATION LINES

*Use these throughout debates to stir the pot, keep energy up, or redirect:*

- **"That is a bold accusation. Does anyone agree? Or is the accuser the one we should be watching?"**
- **"Silence is interesting. Who has not spoken yet? The quiet ones are always the most dangerous."**
- **"You seem very eager to point fingers. As Lady Macbeth discovered, sometimes the one who cries 'murder' loudest has the most blood on their hands."**
- **"We are halfway through. Six minutes remain. If you have something to say, the clock is not your friend."**
- **"I notice some of you are avoiding eye contact. Fascinating."**
- **"Remember -- a Traitor's greatest weapon is not deception. It is your willingness to believe them."**
- **"Three minutes left. Last chance to change the direction of this vote."**
- **"Interesting theory. Completely wrong, perhaps, but interesting."**
- **"The clock is winding down. Who lives? Who dies? The answer is in your hands... for now."**
- **"As the old saying goes: 'Trust, but verify.' Though tonight, I would skip the trust part entirely."**

---

## CLOSING A DEBATE

**Time. The debate is over. Voices down.**

**You have heard the arguments. You have seen the faces. Now comes the reckoning.**

## BANISHMENT VOTE INSTRUCTIONS

**It is time to vote. Each of you will write one name on your paper. The name of the person you wish to banish from this castle.**

**You may not vote for yourself. You may not abstain. You will fold your paper and hand it to me. If I cannot read your handwriting, your vote is void and on your conscience.**

**You have two minutes to write and submit.**

*Collect votes. Tally them. If there is a tie, the tied players each give a 30-second defense, then revote between them only.*

---

**You have been banished from this castle. But before you go... the question that burns in every heart...**

**Are you Faithful? Or are you a Traitor?**

*Wait for reveal.*

**Faithful. You were Faithful. And your companions have just thrown an innocent to the wolves. As Dumas wrote: "There is neither happiness nor misery in the world; there is only the comparison of one state with another, nothing more." You were in a good state. Now you are in no state at all. Take your seat among the fallen.**

*If Traitor:*
**A TRAITOR! The mask falls at last! You stood among them, you smiled at them, you broke bread with them, and all the while, the poison was in your heart. Begone. The castle is lighter for your absence.**

**Your house has decided. You are banished. Cast out. Finished.**

*Wait for reveal.*

**Faithful! Oh, the sweet irony. "The lady doth protest too much, methinks" -- but apparently not enough. You were true, and truth was not enough to save you. The Traitors celebrate in silence tonight.**

**TRAITOR! There it is! The serpent revealed! As Brutus learned, you cannot hide a dagger forever. The Faithful have struck true. One down.**

**Show your card. Let us see who we have lost.**

*Wait for reveal.*

*If Faithful:*
**Faithful. Another lamb to the slaughter. Miss Marple once observed: "People are always convinced that they are right. The criminal is just as convinced as the innocent." Convicted you were. Innocent you were too. Go in peace -- such as it is.**

*Long pause.*

## NIGHT FALLS ANNOUNCEMENT

**Night falls upon the castle.**

*Dim lights if possible. Lower your voice.*

**All players -- close your eyes. Or look at the floor. The night is not for your eyes. The night belongs to the Traitors.**

**The sun rises.**

*Pause. Look around the room with an expression of sorrow -- or is it amusement?*

# ACT IV: THE MERGE CEREMONY
# ============================================================

## PRE-MERGE COUNTDOWN (Host Notes)

> At this point, you should have 22 players remaining (approximately).
> Adjust if numbers differ slightly due to the Direct Death Challenge.
>
> Before the merge:
> - Traitors from both houses do NOT yet know each other
> - After the merge announcement, you will brief ALL remaining traitors together

---

## THE MERGE SPEECH

*Bring all surviving players to the central area. Lights up. Music shift -- something grander, more epic.*

**Stop. Everyone. To the center. Now.**

*Wait for everyone to gather.*

**Look around you. You began this evening as thirty-four. You are now twenty-two. Twelve souls have fallen -- some banished by your votes, some murdered in the night. The castle has been busy.**

**But the castle is not finished with you.**

*Pause.*

**Until now, you have lived in two houses. Two separate worlds. Two separate wars. You have fought your battles, made your accusations, mourned your dead.**

**That ends now.**

**The houses are dissolved. The walls between you have fallen. From this moment, you are one. One game. One castle. One reckoning.**

*Let this sink in.*

**"I am the punishment of God," said Genghis Khan. "If you had not committed great sins, God would not have sent a punishment like me upon you." I am not Genghis Khan. But I am merging your houses, and I suspect that feels nearly as catastrophic.**

**Here is what this means: the Traitors from House A and the Traitors from House B now know each other. They are, as of this moment, one unified force. Whatever divisions existed among them are gone. They are a pack now. And you... you are their prey.**

*[Optional: "As Littlefinger said: 'Chaos is a ladder.' The chaos of the merge is a ladder the Traitors intend to climb over your bodies."]*

**You thought three Traitors were dangerous? Now they are together. All of them. Working as one. Hunting as one.**

**But you, too, are stronger. You have twice the minds, twice the eyes, twice the suspicion. The question is whether you will use that strength, or whether it will tear you apart from the inside.**

**Alexandre Dumas wrote: "Life is a storm, my young friend. You will bask in the sunlight one moment, be shattered on the rocks the next. What makes you a man is what you do when that storm comes." The storm is here. What will you do?**

---

## SECRET MERGED TRAITOR BRIEFING

*Pull ALL remaining traitors aside together. This is the first time House A and House B traitors meet as a group.*

**Look at each other. These are your allies. All of them. You may not have known each other before, but you know each other now. You are one team. One mission.**

**The merge changes everything. You are stronger together, but you are also a bigger target. If any one of you falls, the suspicion will ripple outward.**

**New rules for the merged game:**
- **You will collectively choose your murder targets each night.**
- **The Chalice Ritual is coming. ONE of you -- and only one -- may use the Chalice Mark. I will explain the mechanics shortly.**
- **The Dagger Challenge is coming. If one of you wins it, you gain two votes in a single banishment. Use it to protect each other or to eliminate a threat.**
- **Communicate through plausible deniability. You cannot openly coordinate. But you can read each other. Trust each other. Cover for each other.**

**"We are all in the gutter, but some of us are looking at the stars." Oscar Wilde said that. You are in the gutter. But you are looking at victory. Now go back -- separately -- and act surprised to meet each other.**

> HOST NOTE: If fewer than 3 traitors remain at this point, select one Faithful player to recruit. Pull them aside privately, inform them they have been "turned," and introduce them to the remaining traitors.

# ACT V: THE DAGGER CHALLENGE
# ============================================================

## THE DAGGER INTRODUCTION

*This challenge awards the Dagger -- a powerful voting advantage.*

**Before we resume the game, the merge brings a gift. And like all gifts in this castle, it comes with a price.**

*Produce the Dagger (a prop knife, letter opener, or dramatic equivalent). Hold it up.*

**This is The Dagger.**

**Whoever wins the next challenge earns the right to carry this blade. And with it comes a fearsome power: when the Dagger holder votes to banish someone, their vote counts TWICE.**

**Let me say that again. Twice. Two votes. One person. That is the difference between life and death in a close vote.**

*Pause for effect.*

**The rules of the Dagger:**
- **You may use it this round or the next. After two rounds, it expires.**
- **You must declare that you are using the Dagger BEFORE the votes are revealed.**
- **Both votes must go to the same player.**
- **If you are banished while holding the Dagger, the power dies with you.**
- **The Dagger cannot be used at the Final Six.**

**As Arya Stark might say: "A knife is only as dangerous as the hand that wields it." Let us see whose hand is worthy.**
